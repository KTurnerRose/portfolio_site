PharmaNYC

A website for an independent pharmacy that lets users schedule vaccine appointments and check on prescriptions.

Duration

January 2023 - Febuary 2023

View Project Presentation

Background

This small independent pharmacy based in New York City struggles to schedule and manage vaccine appointments with their current phone and manual system.

How might we establish more efficient and accurate communication between the pharmacists and their clients?

Competitive Audit

Research was conducted on large chain pharmacies and local pharmacies to evaluate what they prioritized for their site, how they used design to establish their brand while providing functionality for as many users as possible, and how they helped users schedule vaccines online.

User Research

To get a sense about the users for the product, customers of the independent pharmacy were interviewed. Those who scheduled vaccines in the past recalled their experience, and those who had not explained their reasons or what parts of the scheduling process dissuaded them. The customers that were interviewed described what they would like to see from a vaccine scheduler website.

Many participants felt the scheduling process was inefficient. Customers who were hard-of-hearing struggled to communicate with pharmacists over the phone, to see print and to handwrite their information on vaccination forms.

Personas

The goals, frustrations and backgrounds of users interviewed were summarized into two user personas.

Ruth Jones

Age: 90
Education: Bachelors

Goals

  • Communicate and schedule vaccine appointments with pharmacy
  • Keep in touch with pharmacists easily

    • Hometown: New York, NY
    Job: Building Committee Board Member
    Years using pharmacy: 25

    Frustrations

  • Is not very tech savvy
  • Is hard-of-hearing, so phone scheduling is difficult

    • A longtime member of her community, Ruth has been a loyal customer of the independent pharmacy. She needs many vaccines in her old age to protect her against getting sick, but both phone and website scheduling pose issues for her.

    Eric Mayorga

    Age: 45
    Education: Masters

    Goals

  • Efficiently schedule vaccine appointments
  • Enter and verify insurance coverage for prescriptions and vaccinations online

    • Hometown: Baltimore, MD
    Job: Finance
    Years using pharmacy: 1

    Frustrations

  • Finds phone scheduling to be antiquated and error-prone
  • In-person vaccine visits take longer when he has to fill out forms instead of completing them in advance

    • Eric is a new member of his community and is trying out this pharmacy. He is time conscious and is frustrated with the current scheduling process for vaccinations. It has dissuaded him from supporting the pharmacy, even though he wants to support small businesses.

    Ideation

    With user interests and the problem in mind, features for KnowGov were connected into a sitemap. The home page is divided into abs for local and federal government articles. The 'Education' page teaches users about local and federal government, the 'Events' page has resources to help users get involved in causes they care about, and the 'User Account' page shows a user's saved articles and events.

    The Crazy Eights method was used to brainstorm layouts for wireframes. Wireframes A through E design for a desktop.

    The digital wireframes addressed user pain points, such as the need for an online vaccine scheduler and a site translation into Spanish, while also integrating prioritized elements from paper wireframes.

    Takeaways

    Continually compare current designs to competitors' final products to assess whether or not the design includes the 'best' elements of these products.

    I applied my user research and my experience working with both customers and pharmacists to design a website that responds to each party's needs.

    I learned how to use Adobe XD to create prototypes, as well as how to use Figma to create sitemaps for a project.

    Brainstorming and extensive iteration on elements are both crucial for developing an accessible theme for all users.
